A Unified Geometry-aware Source Localization and Separation Framework for Ad-hoc Microphone Array

Jingjie Fan, Rongzhi Gu, Yi Luo

Abstract:

An important challenge in multi-channel speech processing for ad-hoc microphone array lies in employing a universal model capable of processing arrays with different geometries or varying numbers of microphones. This requires the model to process inputs of different dimensions and extract array geometry-independent features. Although there are existing end-to-end neural networks designed for ad-hoc microphone array, they have not fully exploited the information provided by the microphone arrays. In this paper, we propose Galatea, a unified geometry-aware source localization and separation framework for ad-hoc microphone array.
